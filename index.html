Diverse Ghats

Varanasi boasts around 87 ghats, each with its own identity. Some are used for bathing and ceremonies, while others are associated with specific legends or historical events. The Assi Ghat, for example, is where the River Assi meets the Ganges, and it's known for its peaceful ambiance and yoga practices.

Architecture and Heritage

Many of these ghats exhibit diverse architectural styles, reflecting different periods of history. Some are adorned with temples, palaces, and ornate buildings, showcasing the rich architectural heritage of Varanasi.

Riverfront Experience

Exploring the ghats by boat offers a unique perspective. It allows you to witness the bustling activity along the riverbanks and observe the ghats' beauty from a serene vantage point.

Rituals and Ceremonies

The ghats are bustling with various activities throughout the day. Pilgrims arrive to perform rituals, offer prayers, and meditate. Devotees also perform Ganga Aarti, a mesmerizing ritual involving fire offerings to the river, held at specific ghats in the evening. The Dashashwamedh Ghat is particularly famous for its grand Ganga Aarti ceremony
